What This Is

One hour that might change
everything that comes after it.

Rocking Life is built on one idea: a man who's been transformed doesn't stay quiet about it. He finds the next man. That's what FORGE groups are — small groups of up to 12 men who meet weekly. Honest conversations. Real accountability. No pretending.

Men in these groups are reading their Bible for the first time in years. Calling their fathers. Praying with their kids. Some are starting their own groups. One man at a time. One fire at a time.

In this webinar, Per Angerstig — founder of Rocking Life — will walk you through how this started, what a FORGE group actually looks like from the inside, and where you might fit in the multiplication model.

In This Webinar

Four things Per will walk you through

01 — How This Started

A fire in Seattle in 1999 and seven men who showed up in 2015. The ember vision that drives everything Rocking Life does.

02 — What a FORGE Group Is

What actually happens inside the room. The 75-minute structure. Why it works when other men's groups don't. What the Holy Spirit does when men stop pretending.

03 — The Multiplication Model

How one group becomes two. How two become four. Why third-generation multiplication is the true measure of success — and what it looks like in practice.

04 — What the Next Step Looks Like for You

This isn't a general presentation. Per will be direct with you about whether this is a fit — and what happens if it is.

Be Honest With Yourself

This is for you if —

  • You've been a Christian for a while and something still feels missing
  • You want to go deeper with other men, not just wider
  • You've thought about leading something but didn't know what
  • You know a man who is struggling and you want to be the one who shows up for him
  • You're in a different geography and have no group to join locally

This is not for you if —

  • You're looking for a program to attend and check off
  • You're looking for someone to hand you a formula
  • You've already decided nothing is going to change
  • You're not ready to think about who you might eventually lead
